About arboriculture

Arboriculture (, from Latin: arboris + society) is the farming, management, and study of private trees, shrubs, vines, and various other seasonal woody plants. The scientific research of arboriculture studies exactly how these plants grow and respond to cultural techniques and to their setting. The practice of arboriculture includes cultural methods such as option, planting, training, fertilizing, parasite and virus control, pruning, shaping, and removal.

About Tree climbing

Tree climbing is an entertainment or practical task containing ascending and moving about in the crowns of trees. A rope, helmet, and harness can be made use of to increase the safety of the mountain climber. Other equipment can additionally be used, depending on the experience and skill of the tree climber. Some tree mountain climbers take unique hammocks called "Treeboats" and Portaledges with them into canopies where they can have an outing or rest. Some tree mountain climbers employ a combination of methods and gear stemmed from rock climbing and caving. These techniques are likewise used to climb up trees for other functions: tree care (arborists), animal rescue, research, and advocacy.

About arborist

An arborist, or (less commonly) arboriculturist, is an expert in the technique of arboriculture, which is the cultivation, management, and research of private trees, shrubs, creeping plants, and other seasonal woody plants in dendrology and horticulture. Arborists normally focus on the health and safety of private plants and trees, rather than handling forests or gathering timber (silviculture or forestry). An arborist's range of work is as a result unique from that of either a forester or a logger.
